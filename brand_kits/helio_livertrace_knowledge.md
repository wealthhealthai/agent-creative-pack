## Product

LiverTrace is an at-home liver health screening test kit for adults 40+. It helps people understand their liver health status before symptoms appear. The user collects a small blood sample at home, sends it in, and receives a report. A physician authorization is included — the user does not need to visit a doctor first.

**Category:** Consumer health / laboratory developed test (LDT)
**Price point:** ~$99
**Website:** livertrace.com

---

## Audience

**Primary:** Adults 40–65 with known or suspected liver risk factors — including:
- Adults with hepatitis B or C history
- Adults with cirrhosis, NAFLD, or fatty liver
- People with a family history of liver disease or liver cancer
- People who are health-proactive and monitor their wellness

**Psychographic:** These are people who are paying attention to their health before something goes wrong. They are not panicked — they are proactive. The emotional state we want to reach: informed empowerment, not fear.

**Secondary:** Adult children of people with liver conditions who are now wondering about their own risk.

**Geography:** US-focused.

---

## Core Value Propositions

1. **Early awareness changes outcomes.** Most liver disease has no symptoms until it is serious. LiverTrace gives you information while options are still widest.
2. **At-home simplicity.** No doctor's appointment required. Physician authorization included.
3. **For people who take their health seriously.** This is not a product for the reactive patient — it's for the proactive one.
4. **Peace of mind OR clarity to act.** Either result (normal or elevated) is valuable.

## What To Say

**Proven emotional angles:**
- "Take control of your liver health" (empowerment, proactive)
- "Know your risk before you need to" (prevention-first framing)
- "Most liver disease has no symptoms until it's serious" (problem awareness)
- "You have risk factors. Now you have a way to act on them." (direct, validating)

**Rational differentiators:**
- At-home (no clinic visit)
- Physician authorization included (removes barrier)
- Designed for adults 40+ with specific risk profiles

**Tone:** Warm, hopeful, empowering. Never frightening. The person in the ad should look like they just received reassurance or clarity — not alarm.

---

## What NOT To Say

**Blocked terms (FDA/regulatory — never use):**
- "detects liver cancer" or "detects cirrhosis"
- "prevents," "cures," "treats," "eliminates"
- "proven to improve outcomes"
- "FDA approved" or "FDA cleared" (it is an LDT, not FDA-cleared)
- "diagnoses" anything

**Allowed alternatives:**
- "helps assess liver health"
- "screens for risk factors"
- "supports early detection" (with care — don't overclaim)
- "know your liver health status"
- "physician-ordered blood test" ✓

**Required disclaimer on every ad:**
> *"LiverTrace is for investigational use only. Not for clinical diagnosis."*

Or, depending on context:
> *"LiverTrace is a laboratory developed test. Physician authorization included. Not for clinical diagnosis."*

---

## Brand Voice

Clinical enough to be credible. Warm enough to be human. Never cold, never alarming. The tone is a knowledgeable friend who happens to know about liver health — not a pharmaceutical ad, not a fear-based PSA. Think: "Take control" not "You might be at risk."

**Imagery that works:**
- Older adults (50s–60s) in warm, natural settings — outdoors, golden light, home environments
- Moments of connection and warmth (couple, patient and doctor/nurse with warmth)
- Full-face, emotionally present — people who look like they've just received good news or reassurance
- Warm golden/amber tones — sunrise/sunset light is on-brand
- The LiverTrace box in foreground when doing product-inclusive ads

**Imagery to avoid:**
- Clinical/sterile hospital settings (too cold)
- Fear-signaling imagery (clutching stomach, worried expression)
- Stock photo aesthetics (too generic)
- Anything that looks like a pharma ad from 2005
